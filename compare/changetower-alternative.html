Where DiffGoblin wins

AI that explains what changed and why it matters

ChangeTower highlights changes. DiffGoblin explains them. When a competitor adds a new pricing tier, you don't get "text changed on line 47" — you get "New Growth tier added at $149/mo between Pro and Enterprise, suggesting a move upmarket." For competitive intelligence, that context is everything.

Noise filtering that actually works

ChangeTower's visual diffs catch everything — including cookie banners cycling, date stamps updating, and ad slots rotating. You end up training your brain to ignore alerts, which defeats the purpose. DiffGoblin's AI distinguishes "the copyright year changed" from "they raised prices 20%."

Simpler for competitive monitoring

ChangeTower was built for compliance and archiving — tracking TOS changes, maintaining website history records, legal discovery. It's great at that. But if your primary use case is "tell me when my competitor changes their pricing/features/messaging," DiffGoblin is purpose-built for that workflow.

Where ChangeTower wins

Website archiving

ChangeTower's archive feature saves complete snapshots of pages over time — useful for legal compliance, regulatory monitoring, and evidence preservation. DiffGoblin focuses on diffs and summaries, not full archival.

HTML-level change detection

ChangeTower monitors both visual appearance AND HTML source code. If an invisible tracking pixel gets added or a meta tag changes, ChangeTower catches it. DiffGoblin is screenshot-based, so invisible changes aren't detected.

Established platform

ChangeTower has years of history, more integrations, and more flexibility for complex monitoring setups. DiffGoblin is new — fast-moving and opinionated, but newer.

The verdict

Pick DiffGoblin if: You're a PM, marketer, or competitive intel analyst who wants to know when competitors change pricing, features, or positioning — and wants an AI-generated summary in your inbox, not just a highlighted screenshot.

Pick ChangeTower if: You need website archiving for compliance, legal monitoring, or you want HTML-level change detection for technical monitoring use cases.

The TL;DR: ChangeTower answers "what changed on this page?" DiffGoblin answers "what does this change mean for my business?"
